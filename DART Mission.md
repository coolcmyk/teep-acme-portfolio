# DART Mission

NASA's Double Asteroid Redirection Test — the first full-scale test of kinetic impactor technology for planetary defense.

### Mission Overview
- **Launch**: November 2021
- **Impact**: September 26, 2022
- **Target**: Dimorphos (secondary of binary asteroid 65803 Didymos)
- **Impactor mass**: ~570 kg
- **Impact velocity**: ~6.6 km/s

### Key Results
- **Orbital period change**: −33.0 ± 1.0 min (Thomas et al., 2023)
- Expected for perfectly inelastic case: ~7 min
- **β factor** (momentum enhancement): much higher than predicted
- Impact may have caused global reshaping rather than localized cratering (Raducan et al., 2024)
- Debate ongoing whether extreme reshaping definitively occurred (Rivkin & Chabot, 2026)

### Scientific Context
- Dimorphos likely a weak rubble pile → global deformation rather than cratering
- Ejecta contributed additional momentum
- Experimental data guides future kinetic impactor design

### Hera Mission (ESA, upcoming)
- Will investigate the DART impact outcome
- Characterize Dimorphos post-impact
- Resolve reshaping debate
- Validate computational models

### Relevance to MPM
- MPM's explicit fragment tracking and contact handling could provide better predictions for DART-like scenarios
- Can model rubble-pile interior response to impact
- Validated framework contributes to planetary defense simulation capabilities
