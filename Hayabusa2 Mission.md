# Hayabusa2 Mission

JAXA's asteroid sample-return mission to (162173) Ryugu, which included an artificial impact experiment.

### Mission Overview
- **Launch**: December 2014
- **Arrival at Ryugu**: June 2018
- **SCI impact**: April 2019 (Small Carry-on Impactor)
- **Sample return**: December 2020

### SCI Impact Experiment
- Created artificial crater on Ryugu's surface
- **Observed crater**: 17.6 ± 0.7 m rim-to-rim (14.5 ± 0.8 m from original horizon)
- **Predicted crater**: 2 to 10 m (assuming strength-controlled surface)
- Crater far exceeded predictions (Arakawa et al., 2020)

### Implications
- Gravity-dominated rather than strength-dominated cratering regime
- Ryugu surface material weaker and/or more porous than expected
- Surface likely controlled by regolith with low cohesion
- Rubble-pile nature confirmed

### Relevance to Impact Simulations
- Both DART and Hayabusa2 impacts exceeded predictions → underscores need for better understanding of asteroid physical properties
- Accurate shock-physics codes essential for interpreting mission data
- Material models must capture diverse behaviors from laboratory to asteroid scales
- MPM could contribute to modeling impacts on rubble-pile and regolith-covered surfaces
