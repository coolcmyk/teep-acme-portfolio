# Rubble-Pile Asteroids

Asteroids composed of gravitationally bound fragments rather than a single coherent monolith. Represent the most common internal structure for small asteroids.

### Characteristics
- Aggregate of boulders, gravel, and dust held together by gravity
- High porosity (often > 40%)
- Low bulk strength (dominantly gravity-dominated)
- Irregular shapes
- Formed through catastrophic disruption and reaccumulation

### Examples
- **Ryugu** (162173): visited by Hayabusa2; rubble-pile structure confirmed
- **Bennu** (101955): visited by OSIRIS-REx; very low bulk density
- **Itokawa** (25143): visited by Hayabusa; classic rubble pile
- **Dimorphos**: DART target; weak rubble pile may explain large orbital change

### Impact Behavior
- Shock waves propagate differently than in monoliths
- Porosity collapses, absorbing impact energy
- Complicates crater formation predictions
- DART impact on Dimorphos: much larger momentum enhancement than predicted for non-porous body
