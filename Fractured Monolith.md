# Fractured Monolith

A hypothesis for the internal structure of certain asteroids: a large, intact fragment (shard) from a parent body that remains structurally coherent despite extensive internal fracturing.

### Key Example: (433) Eros
- Near-Earth S-type asteroid
- Dimensions: 34.4 × 11.2 × 11.2 km
- Effective diameter: 16.84 km
- Debate: fractured monolith vs rubble pile

### Evidence for Fractured Monolith
- Surface features: grooves and ridges consistent with global fracturing
- Tonge et al. (2016): strong internal flaw distribution needed to reproduce Eros's surface features from major impacts
- NEAR spacecraft observations: evidence of coherent structure

### Evidence for Rubble Pile
- Ballouz et al. (2025): seismic data analysis suggests rubble-pile-like interior properties
- If Eros were a monolith, Solar System age worth of collisions should have shattered it

### This Paper's Contribution
The MPM b2 simulation (high damage strength, modified Lundborg model) produced a large, Eros-like remnant:
- Prolate shape, maximum dimension 16 km
- Resembles Eros in both size and overall shape
- Demonstrates that a single catastrophic impact on a strong parent body can directly produce an Eros-sized shattered-but-not-disrupted body

### Resolution
A fractured monolith generated from a catastrophic impact could exhibit seismic properties similar to a rubble pile due to extensive internal fracturing, potentially reconciling both observations.
