# Hypervelocity Impact

A collision where the impact velocity significantly exceeds the speed of sound in the target material, producing extreme pressures and material behavior.

### Characteristics
- Impact velocities: km/s scale (3-5 km/s typical for asteroid impacts)
- Produces shock waves, phase changes, melting, vaporization
- Material strength often negligible compared to dynamic pressures
- Time scales: microseconds to seconds

### In Asteroid Science
- Primary mechanism for asteroid evolution and family formation
- Causes fragmentation (short timescale, shock physics)
- Followed by gravitational reaccumulation (long timescale, N-body)
- Relevant for planetary defense (kinetic impactor deflection)

### Laboratory Experiments
- Nakamura & Fujiwara (1991): basalt targets, nylon projectiles at ~3.2 km/s
- Key benchmark for validating shock-physics codes
