# Planetary Defense

Strategies and technologies to protect Earth from potentially hazardous asteroid impacts. Kinetic impactor deflection is a primary technique.

### DART Mission (NASA, 2022)
- First full-scale test of kinetic impactor technology
- Target: Dimorphos (secondary of binary asteroid Didymos)
- Impact velocity: ~6.6 km/s
- Result: orbital period changed by −33.0 ± 1.0 min (far exceeding the expected ~7 min)
- Implication: momentum enhancement factor (β) was much higher than predicted
- Reason: impact ejecta contributed additional momentum

### Why Predictions Were Off
- Assumed simple strength-dominated cratering
- Dimorphos may be a weak rubble pile
- Impact may have caused global reshaping rather than localized cratering
- Accurate modeling requires understanding of target internal structure and material response

### Role of Shock-Physics Codes
- Predict impact outcomes for planetary defense planning
- Model momentum transfer efficiency (β factor)
- Guide mission design (impactor mass, velocity, target selection)

### MPM Contributions
- Better contact and boundary condition handling
- More accurate fragment and ejecta tracking
- Ability to model complex target interiors (rubble piles, regolith layers)
- Validated framework for future planetary defense simulations

### Related Missions
- **Hayabusa2** (JAXA): SCI impactor created artificial crater on Ryugu — crater far exceeded predictions
- **Hera** (ESA, upcoming): Will investigate DART impact outcome at Didymos/Dimorphos
