# Asteroid Families

Groups of asteroids with similar orbital elements (semi-major axis, eccentricity, inclination), believed to share a common collisional origin from a single parent body.

### Formation
1. Catastrophic disruption of a parent body
2. Fragments ejected with a velocity field
3. Gravitational reaccumulation of some fragments
4. Remaining fragments dispersed in orbital element space

### First Complete Numerical Reproduction
Michel et al. (2001): Sequential fragmentation (SPH) + gravitational reaccumulation (N-body) successfully reproduced the formation of asteroid families.

### Types of Families
- **Catastrophic disruption families**: from complete parent body destruction — many small bodies
- **Cratering families**: from large cratering events — largest remnant survives
- Properties depend on: parent body size, composition, porosity, impact energy

### Role of Material Properties
- Different material models produce different family types (Michel et al., 2001-2004)
- Compaction structure of parent body affects outcome
- Weibull parameters (strength distribution) critically determine fragment size distribution

### MPM's Potential
- More accurate fragment shapes → better initial conditions for reaccumulation
- More realistic fragment size distributions from improved material models
- Can model complex parent body interiors (layered, rubble-pile)
